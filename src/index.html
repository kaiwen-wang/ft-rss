General thoughts on news:

Staying informed yet not getting distracted is important. I know that 90% of the news is garbage, but I still find it necessary to roughly know what's going on. For some people, maybe they can browse it once in the morning and let it be. If I look at the front page, I'll inevitably end up scrolling around due to the big pictures and headlines. Getting only the most read stories is a good alternative.

In my view, a Bloomberg Terminal-like interface is optimal. Focusing your attention is a core part of succeeding in the modern economy. That's why I use a RSS reader and curate my sources from informative blogs, personal websites, substacks, legal news, and so on.

There aren't any other newspapers I find interesting or relevant. Sources like La Tribune, Süddeutsche Zeitung, and Straits Times seem too regional. Similar to the FT include the WSJ, Economist, and Bloomberg, but they don't quite seem the same quality. The headlines hit lacklustrely, but lately the FT has been like that as well, especially for geopolitical concerns and how they turn off comments. We'll wait and see.

I feel like the most read on Nikkei Asia and Zhihu may bring in interesting new perspectives, and they would be the target of my next RSS Most Read project.
